Hello

I am a doctoral student in the Baldwin Lab at UC Berkeley. My research interests are in the empirical and theoretical aspects of plant evolution, computational phylogenetics, and conservation informatics.

Plant systematics and evolution

I've been researching the phylogeny and divergence times of the North American Rhodiola (Crassulaceae) using both nuclear and chloroplast DNA. I am particularly interested in using phylogenomic approaches to resolve the evolutionary history of hybridization within plant groups.

Computational phylogenetics

How do we model the evolution of traits responsible for diversification on molecular phylogenies? How can we infer phylogenetic networks to better understand reticulate evolution? I'm very interested in the theoretical and computational challenges involved in statistical phylogenetic inference.

Conservation informatics

Working with non-profit and volunteer conservation groups, I have developed open source web-based decision support tools to help document and plan ecological restoration projects. I'm interested in the ways information technology can be used to make spatially explicit conservation management more collaborative and transparent. Can we build useful tools for conservationists that incorporate metrics of phylogenetic diversity?
