Will Freyman



Hello

I am a doctoral student in the Baldwin Lab at UC Berkeley. My research interests revolve around the empirical and theoretical aspects of plant evolution, computational phylogenetics, and biodiversity informatics.

Plant systematics and evolution

I've been researching the phylogeny and divergence times of the North American Rhodiola (Crassulaceae) using both nuclear and chloroplast DNA. I am particularly interested in using phylogenomic approaches to resolve the evolutionary history of hybridization within plant groups.

Computational phylogenetics

How do we model the evolution of traits responsible for diversification on molecular phylogenies? How can we infer phylogenetic networks to better understand reticulate evolution? I'm very interested in the theoretical and computational challenges involved in statistical phylogenetic inference.

Biodiversity informatics

Working with non-profit and volunteer conservation groups, I have developed open source web-based tools to help manage and assess conservation projects. I'm interested in building tools that visualize biodiversity data and help make conservation management more collaborative and transparent. Can we build useful tools for conservationists that incorporate metrics of phylogenetic diversity?
